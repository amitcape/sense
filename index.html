Current Projects

CommSense JCAS Systems

In one of the early patents in the domain of joint communication and sensing (JCAS), we pioneered the use of channel equalisation signals to sense the environment (an innovation which we patented in 2015. We have shown its application in a range of applications starting from detecting wheel-chairs in public places to detecting drones in cluttered backgrounds. If you want to design a system to detect a target or situation of interest, our innovation may mostly be of use.

Sensor Calibration using AI

This project deployed a co-located sensor platform at Cape Point to improve the performance of low-cost air quality sensors. Leveraging expertise in sensor design and blind calibration, advanced AI methods were developed to enhance measurement reliability without relying on reference instruments. The work has been internationally recognised, with invited tutorials delivered at flagship conferences such as IEEE I2MTC 2024, and has led to a patent on innovative calibration approaches (WO2024092293A1). Results have also been published in IEEE I2MTC and IEEE TIM.
